## Code licence and brand rights are different

The Community Edition is available under GPL-3.0-or-later. That licence grants
rights in the code; it does not grant trademark or brand rights. A fork or
redistribution must comply with the GPL and should not present itself as an
official Netbrowse Media (PTY) LTD product.

## Permitted factual use

You may use the names in a factual, non-misleading way to describe:

- an unmodified copy of Netbrowse Voice Community Edition;
- compatibility with Netbrowse Voice; or
- the historical origin of a fork.

Keep copyright and licence notices intact, and make the relationship clear.
For example: “Based on Netbrowse Voice Community Edition” is normally clearer
than naming a modified fork simply “Netbrowse Voice”.

## Uses that require permission

Do not use the Netbrowse Media or Netbrowse Voice name, logo, domain identity or
visual presentation in a way that suggests that a modified build, hosted
service, commercial module or support offering is official, endorsed or
affiliated with Netbrowse Media (PTY) LTD.

Modified forks should use their own product name and replace official logos and
prominent branded presentation. Do not imply certification, partnership or
approval unless it has been expressly granted in writing.

is a practical community guideline, not legal advice or a substitute for formal
trademark registration and enforcement.
